About me

My full name is David Ignacio Iglesias Echevarria (Spanish naming customs use one last name from each parent (Iglesias - dad, Echevarria - mom)). My name is pronounced dah-beed - no need to feel bad if you've used the English pronunciation (unless I've explicitly told you the correct one lol). I'm originally from Lleida, a beautiful, historical town in Catalonia, Spain, and lived in Barcelona and Boulder, Colorado, before moving to the Bay Area.

My professional interests lie in the areas of autonomy and artificial intelligence, specializing mainly in perception, localization, and mapping. I think robotics (as in, embodied intelligence) has the potential to positively transform societies and economies throughout the world, and so spending time accelerating progress in this field is a worthy thing.

I got my Master's degree in Robotics from the University of Colorado, Boulder, where I did research in cooperative pose estimation for multi-robot systems in bandwidth-constrained environments, under Prof. Nisar Ahmed (check out his research, it's cool!) Before that, I did my undergrad in Aerospace Engineering at UPC-BarcelonaTech, motivated by my love of space exploration and systems that operate in the real world under physical constraints.

My CV is available upon request. If you want to contact me for professional reasons, you can shoot me an email.

Professional Experience

Always learning...

  • Sr. Software Engineer - Localization & Mapping, XPeng Motors, Santa Clara, US
    Dec 2020 - present
    • This has been my longest stint to date by far (still going!), so there's too much to cover in detail (feel free to ask me for additional info, though). During my time here, I've worked on both improving legacy solutions as well as prototyping + testing + deploying new ones. This has been mostly in two areas. One, localization, where I've worked on absolute positioning ( using GPS/GNSS), dead-reckoning (in GPS-denied environments), and calibration. Two, mapping, where I've worked on road surface estimation and, more generally, building reliable and accurate local maps around the vehicle. I've also spent big chunks of time defining, implementing, and maintaining metrics for both pose smoothness and accuracy, and for lane line quality and consistency. This latter stuff is sometimes glossed over, as it is less glamorous than other parts of the stack, but in my opinion is one of the most critical, less well understood ones.
