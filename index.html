About me

My full name is David Ignacio Iglesias Echevarria. My name is pronounced dah-beed, emphasis on the second syllable - no need to feel bad if you've pronounced it differently (unless I've explicitly told you the correct one lol). I'm originally from Lleida, a beautiful, historical town in Catalonia, Spain, and lived in Barcelona and Boulder, Colorado, before moving to the Bay Area.

My professional interests lie in the areas of autonomy and artificial intelligence, specializing mainly in perception, localization, and mapping. I think robotics (as in, embodied intelligence) has the potential to positively transform societies and economies throughout the world, so spending time accelerating progress in the field is a generally worthy pursuit.

I got my Master's degree in Robotics from the University of Colorado, Boulder, where I did research in cooperative pose estimation for multi-robot systems in bandwidth-constrained environments, under Prof. Nisar Ahmed (check out his research, it's cool!) Before that, I did my undergrad in Aerospace Engineering at UPC-BarcelonaTech, driven by my love of space exploration and systems that operate in the real world under physical constraints.

    As a member of the Localization & Mapping team, I worked on both improving legacy solutions as well as prototyping + testing + deploying new ones.

    On the localization side, I worked on absolute positioning (using GPS/GNSS), dead-reckoning (in GPS-denied environments), and calibration. This was pretty early on in my time here, so the code base was pretty green and a lot of the stuff was implemented from scratch - it was fun.

    All throughout, in parallel, I spent big chunks of time defining, implementing, and maintaining metrics for both pose smoothness and accuracy, and for lane line quality and temporal/geometric consistency. This latter stuff is sometimes seen as less exciting, but imo is critical and every single person should write their own metrics and tests, since it forces you to think about what "good" is at a low level.
